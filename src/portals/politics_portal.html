My views on politics have mostly been influenced by the Italian Elite School, that is, Vilfredo Pareto, Gaetano Mosca, and Robert Michels. This school was founded on the thought of Machiavelli, and given a notable boost by James Burnham.

The Prince

The Prince by Machiavelli is a classic text, and is among the first true political science texts ever written. That is, Machiavelli was attempting to analyse politics in a scientific manner, the study of the use and distribution of power. The book was written as a gift to the de Medici family; Machiavelli hoped they could unite Italy under one banner to stop the smaller Italian states being bullied by larger ones.

The Machiavellians

The Machiavellians by James Burnham is a modern revamp of Machiavellian thought and covers the thoughts of Italian Elite School theorists, as well as some contributions from Sorel. This is a seminal work in understanding modern politics, and there is no degree to which I would not stop recommending this work.
